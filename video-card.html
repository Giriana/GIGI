Video & Expansion Cards

What are Video & Expansion Cards?

These are printed circuit boards that can be inserted into an expansion slot on a computer motherboard to add functionality to a computer system. The most common is the graphics card (GPU), but others include sound cards, network cards, and storage controllers.

Types of Expansion Cards

  • Graphics Cards (GPU): Handles rendering images to display
  • Sound Cards: Provides enhanced audio capabilities
  • Network Interface Cards (NIC): For wired or wireless networking
  • Storage Controllers: Adds additional SATA, SAS, or NVMe ports
  • Capture Cards: For recording video input

Graphics Card Specifications

  • GPU Chip: The processor (NVIDIA RTX, AMD RX, etc.)
  • VRAM: Video memory capacity (4GB, 8GB, etc.)
