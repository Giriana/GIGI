Computer Cases & Form Factors

What is a Computer Case?

A computer case, also known as a chassis, tower, system unit, or cabinet, is the enclosure that contains most of the components of a personal computer (usually excluding the display, keyboard, and mouse).

Functions of a Computer Case

  • Protects components from physical damage and dust
  • Provides structural support for internal components
  • Helps with cooling by providing space for fans and airflow
  • Shields against electromagnetic interference
  • Provides ports and buttons for user interaction

Form Factors

Computer cases come in different sizes and layouts known as form factors. The most common include:

  • ATX: The standard size for most desktop PCs (12 x 9.6 inches)
  • Micro-ATX: A smaller version of ATX (9.6 x 9.6 inches)
  • Mini-ITX: Very compact form factor (6.7 x 6.7 inches)
  • E-ATX: Extended ATX for high-end systems (12 x 13 inches)

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Overheating: Ensure proper airflow and clean dust filters regularly
  • Noise: Check for loose components or failing case fans
  • Component doesn't fit: Verify case supports your motherboard and GPU size
  • Front ports not working: Check internal connections to motherboard
