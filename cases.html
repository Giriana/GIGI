Computer Cases & Form Factors

The enclosure that contains most of the components of a computer

Description and Function

The computer case is the enclosure that contains most of the components of a computer (usually excluding the display, keyboard, and mouse). Cases are usually constructed from steel, aluminum, and plastic.

Common form factors include:

  • Full Tower: Largest case type (20-30 inches tall)
  • Mid Tower: Most common size (15-20 inches tall)
  • Mini Tower: Compact size (12-15 inches tall)
  • SFF: Small Form Factor (under 12 inches)

Key functions:

  1. Protects components from dust and damage
  2. Provides structural support
  3. Manages airflow and cooling
  4. Reduces electromagnetic interference

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Overheating: Clean dust filters, ensure proper airflow
  • Noise: Check for loose components or failing fans
  • Component doesn't fit: Verify case compatibility before purchase
