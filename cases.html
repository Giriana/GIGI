Computer Cases & Form Factors

The foundation that houses and protects all your computer components

About Computer Cases

The computer case is the enclosure that contains most of the components of a computer. It provides physical protection and cooling for internal components while also affecting the system's aesthetics and noise levels.

Key Functions:

  • Protects internal components from physical damage and dust
  • Provides structural support for components
  • Helps with cooling through fan mounts and airflow design
  • Shields against electromagnetic interference
  • Organizes components and cables

Common Form Factors:

  • ATX: The most common form factor (12 x 9.6 inches)
  • Micro-ATX: Smaller version of ATX (9.6 x 9.6 inches)
  • Mini-ITX: Compact form factor (6.7 x 6.7 inches)
  • E-ATX: Extended ATX for high-end systems

Troubleshooting Tips

Issue: Computer overheating

Solution: Ensure proper airflow by cleaning dust filters, organizing cables, and adding more case fans if needed.

Issue: Components not fitting

Solution: Verify case supports your motherboard form factor and has enough clearance for components like CPU coolers and GPUs.
